# Phase 10: Trusted Federation and Public-Interest Pools

## Objective

Extend Overrid beyond one private swarm while keeping providers known, policies enforceable, and resource donations accountable.

This phase brings in universities, companies, research groups, family/community clouds, and trusted partner swarms before opening low-sensitivity public supply.

## Depends On

- Phase 9 deployable app platform.
- Phase 7 grid-resident backbone.
- Phase 5 ORU and Seal Ledger accounting.
- Phase 4 verification and policy.

## Build Order

1. Define federation participant types.
2. Build federation templates.
3. Add cross-tenant Overgrant policies.
4. Add verified purpose tags.
5. Build public-interest grant pools.
6. Add federation billing and dispute boundaries.
7. Prove one trusted partner swarm.
8. Prove one public-interest pool.

## Workstream 1: Federation Participant Types

Support known participants:

- University.
- Company.
- Research lab.
- Nonprofit.
- Family or community cloud.
- Trusted partner swarm.
- Government or public service partner where legally appropriate.

Each participant needs identity verification, operator records, resource contribution scope, policy boundaries, and dispute contact.

## Workstream 2: Federation Templates

Create templates for:

- Capacity donation.
- Capacity sale at accountable near-cost or agreed terms.
- Research-only pool.
- Education-only pool.
- Internal organization swarm.
- Partner-only overflow.
- Emergency or disaster-response pool.

Templates should specify eligible workloads, data sensitivity, provider obligations, reporting, and accounting behavior.

## Workstream 3: Cross-Tenant Overgrant

Extend Overgrant for:

- Grant source account.
- Eligible tenants or identities.
- Purpose tags.
- Resource dimensions.
- Time windows.
- Quotas.
- Fairness rules.
- Abuse controls.
- Reporting.

Grants are programmable resource allocation, not speculative token drops.

## Workstream 4: Verified Purpose Tags

Start with stewarded tags:

- Science.
- Education.
- Medical.
- Opensource.
- Climate.
- Public infrastructure.

Tags should have eligibility criteria and evidence requirements. A workload claiming a purpose tag must be able to show why it qualifies.

## Workstream 5: Public-Interest Pools

Create pool mechanics:

- Pool account.
- Contributed resources.
- Eligible grantees.
- Per-grantee fairness.
- Quotas.
- Abuse throttles.
- Usage reports.
- Outcome reports where appropriate.
- Renewal and revocation.

Surplus from native services can later route into these pools through central AI stewardship rules.

## Workstream 6: Federation Billing And Disputes

Define boundaries:

- Who pays or sponsors usage.
- Who receives provider earnings.
- What gets held during disputes.
- Which tenant owns logs and evidence.
- Which policy applies.
- How corrections are recorded.

Every cross-tenant job should remain traceable through evidence, usage, and settlement.

## Validation

- Known organization shares approved low-risk capacity under explicit policy.
- Cross-tenant grant allows an eligible workload and denies an ineligible one.
- Public-interest pool enforces purpose tag, quota, and fairness.
- Federation usage creates ORU/Seal Ledger records.
- Disputes can be investigated from stored evidence.

## Exit Gate

Phase 10 is complete when trusted external capacity can join Overrid under enforceable, auditable, purpose-aware rules.

## Handoff To Phase 11

Phase 11 admits unknown or semi-trusted public providers only for tightly bounded low-sensitivity workloads.
